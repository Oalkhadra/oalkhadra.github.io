About

I am a data scientist on GEICO's pricing decision science team. I earned an M.S. in Applied Data Science from the University of Southern California and a B.S. in Systems Engineering from George Washington University with a minor in statistics.

My work sits at the intersection of stochastic modeling and sequential decision-making; building systems that not only forecast under uncertainty but act on those forecasts. Recent applications span auto insurance risk, probabilistic weather forecasting for prediction markets, and noisy geospatial attribution.

Research Interests

I am interested in the dynamics of systems that evolve under uncertainty and in the decisions one should make given imperfect models of them. This pulls me toward stochastic processes, sequential decision-making, and the calibration of probabilistic forecasts. Having approached these questions through geospatial inference, insurance risk, and weather-driven prediction markets, I am drawn most immediately to financial markets; prices encode beliefs, outcomes are observable, and miscalibration has a cost.
