Research Interests

I work on probabilistic modeling under uncertainty, with a focus on calibrated forecasting and decision-making in noisy, high-dimensional settings. My recent work spans point-of-interest attribution from sparse GPS trajectories, probabilistic forecast evaluation in prediction markets, and stochastic modeling of policyholder behavior at scale. I am especially interested in problems where principled uncertainty quantification meaningfully changes downstream decisions — in operations research, financial mathematics, and spatial statistics.

Research & Selected Work

Point-of-interest attribution under spatial uncertainty

With Cyrus Shahabi and the InfoLab group, USC · 2024–2025

Co-developed a transformer-based framework for assigning noisy GPS trajectories to candidate POIs under spatial and temporal uncertainty. Reconstructed proprietary probabilistic baselines — SafeGraph's multi-stage KDE pipeline and Nishida's ILP formulation with spatiotemporal priors — from limited documentation to establish reproducible benchmarks. Built a fault-tolerant ETL pipeline processing 30M+ geospatial records.

paper code

Probabilistic forecasting and prediction market inefficiency

Independent research · 2025–present

Investigated whether temperature prediction markets are well-calibrated relative to numerical weather prediction ensembles. Built a probabilistic forecast model with Student-t residual distributions and multi-source NWP features across 20 cities; validated selective forecasting edge via PIT (KS=0.033) and Diebold-Mariano (p=0.006) against market-implied distributions on traded contracts. Live deployment ongoing for ongoing calibration analysis.

code available on request

Volatility forecasting for delta-neutral options strategies

Independent research · 2025

Built a volatility forecasting framework comparing XGBoost against GARCH(1,1) and historical baselines on five years of SPY data; Diebold-Mariano confirmed XGBoost outperforming GARCH (p=0.004) with 21% lower RMSE. Implemented a delta-neutral options backtester with Black-Scholes pricing, daily delta rebalancing, and regime-conditional variance risk premium estimation.

code

Policyholder defection and stochastic attrition modeling

GEICO Pricing · 2025–present

Developed an XGBoost model on 150M+ policyholder records predicting defection probability as a function of premium changes and customer features. Built a stochastic simulation of policyholder attrition under rate-change scenarios with an interactive dashboard, now used to inform pricing decisions across states representing over $100M in annual written premium.

Economic loss modeling under interdependent sector disruption

George Washington University · 2022–2023

Built a dynamic inoperability input-output (Leontief) model to quantify cascading economic losses across interdependent sectors facing drought. Conducted sensitivity analysis over policy intervention scenarios with expert-elicited parameters; identified interventions reducing potential losses by an estimated 67% (~$5.36B). Published in Environment Systems and Decisions.
