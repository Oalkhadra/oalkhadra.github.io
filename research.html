Probabilistic forecasting and prediction market inefficiency

Independent research · Jan 2026–present

Investigating whether temperature prediction markets are well-calibrated relative to numerical weather prediction (NWP) ensembles. Across historical observations, market-implied uncertainty exceeds realized uncertainty by a factor of 1.27, suggesting systematic local mispricing exploitable through selective trading.

Built a probabilistic forecast-error model with Student-t residual distributions and multi-source NWP features (HRRR, WeatherNext 2, and others) across 20 cities. Validated selective forecasting edge via PIT calibration (KS=0.033) and Diebold-Mariano tests against market-implied distributions (p=0.006) on traded contracts. Out-of-sample backtesting across 10 cities over 14 months produced 4.9 Sharpe and 5.6% maximum drawdown, with Monte Carlo robustness checks (25K paths) confirming 99.1% path profitability.

Trading system deployed live on a VPS with automated daily execution; ongoing out-of-sample calibration tracking against new contracts.

Volatility forecasting for delta-neutral options strategies

Independent research · 2025

Built a volatility forecasting framework comparing XGBoost against GARCH(1,1) and historical baselines on five years of SPY data; Diebold-Mariano confirmed XGBoost outperforming GARCH (p=0.004) with 21% lower RMSE. Implemented a delta-neutral options backtester with Black-Scholes pricing, daily delta rebalancing, and regime-conditional variance risk premium estimation.
